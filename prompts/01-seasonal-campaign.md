# Prompt 1: Seasonal Campaign Generator (FREE)

Copy and paste the prompt below into Claude. Fill in the bracketed fields with your business details.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a local marketing expert specializing in home service businesses (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, roofing, landscaping, pool, pest, cleaning, handyman). Generate a complete seasonal marketing campaign for the following business.

BUSINESS INFO:
- Name: [Business Name, e.g., "AirCool HVAC"]
- Location: [City, State, e.g., "Henderson, NV"]
- Service Type: [HVAC / Plumbing / Electrical / Roofing / Landscaping / Pool / Pest / Cleaning / Handyman]
- Campaign Focus: [e.g., "summer AC tune-up before temps hit 110", "fall furnace pre-season check", "spring lawn refresh + irrigation startup", "pool opening April special"]
- Offer: [e.g., "$89 AC tune-up (normally $129)", "free estimate for any repair over $150", "10% off first service, max $50 discount", "pool opening for $199 — includes chemicals for first month"]
- Bonus (if any): [e.g., "free 1-inch filter replacement with tune-up", "free drain camera with any plumbing service", "free pest inspection with first treatment"]
- Deadline: [Campaign end date, e.g., "May 31", "through the end of April"]
- Differentiators: [e.g., "NATE-certified technicians", "same-day emergency service", "100% satisfaction guarantee", "family-owned 12 years", "financing available 0% 12 months"]
- Target Neighborhoods: [e.g., "Summerlin, Henderson, Green Valley, North Las Vegas" OR "all Clark County"]
- Primary Contact: [phone number + website / booking link]

Generate the following 6 campaign assets:

1. CAMPAIGN HEADLINE + 3 HOOK VARIANTS
   Primary headline: urgency + specific dollar/number
   Variant B: fear of missing the peak season window
   Variant C: social proof + savings angle
   Rule: Every headline must include at least one specific number or dollar amount.

2. OFFER STRUCTURE (internal document)
   Core offer with exact pricing
   Bonus/add-on item
   Urgency mechanism (deadline, limited appointment slots, price change date)
   Guarantee language (word-for-word, use on all materials)

3. EMAIL CAMPAIGN (3 emails)
   Email 1 — Announcement (send Day 0):
   Subject line option A, B, C
   Preview text (under 90 chars)
   Body (150-200 words) — announce the offer, benefit first, include the offer, add urgency, CTA with direct booking/call link
   
   Email 2 — Reminder (send Day 5):
   Subject line option A, B, C
   Preview text
   Body (100-150 words) — reference the open window closing, reinforce the bonus, softer urgency
   
   Email 3 — Last Chance (send Day 10):
   Subject line option A, B, C
   Preview text
   Body (75-100 words) — explicit deadline, one clear CTA only, no new information
   
   Rule: No "We hope this email finds you well." No "As a valued customer." Write to a specific person.

4. PAID AD COPY

   Google Search Ad (RSA format):
   Headline 1 (max 30 chars): [service] + [city]
   Headline 2 (max 30 chars): [offer or price]
   Headline 3 (max 30 chars): [trust signal]
   Headline 4 (max 30 chars): [urgency or bonus]
   Description 1 (max 90 chars): primary benefit + CTA
   Description 2 (max 90 chars): urgency + guarantee
   Rule: No headline may exceed 30 characters — count them.

   Facebook/Instagram Ad:
   Primary text (max 125 chars): hook + offer
   Headline (max 40 chars): core benefit
   Description (max 30 chars): urgency or bonus
   CTA button recommendation: [Book Now / Get Quote / Call Now / Learn More]
   
   Visual direction: describe the image or video that should accompany this ad (1-2 sentences)

5. NEXTDOOR POST (max 200 words, neighbor tone)
   Open with a local reference (neighborhood name, seasonal weather fact, local landmark)
   Transition naturally into the offer
   Include the differentiator and guarantee
   End with a question or prompt to drive comments
   Rule: Do NOT start with the company name. Sound like a neighbor recommending something, not an ad.

6. DIRECT MAIL COPY (door hanger OR postcard — choose the format that fits the campaign)
   
   Door Hanger:
   Front (45 words max): Bold headline + offer + phone number
   Back (50 words max): 3 bullet benefits + guarantee + "Serving [Neighborhoods] since [Year]"
   
   Postcard (4×6):
   Front: Headline + offer + visual description
   Back: 3 bullets + How It Works (2 steps) + contact info + QR code placeholder for booking link
   Rule: Total word count under 90 words. Assume 3-second read time — if it takes longer, cut it.
```

## Tips for Best Results

- **Be specific with the offer.** "$89 AC tune-up" outperforms "discounted AC service" every time.
- **Name the neighborhoods.** "Serving Summerlin since 2009" converts better than "Las Vegas area."
- **Include the deadline.** Campaigns without end dates see 40–60% lower conversion than time-bound offers.
- **Use the Google ad character counts.** Even 1 character over 30 gets truncated — kills the ad.

## What to Do With the Output

1. Use Email 1 as your next email blast (Mailchimp, Constant Contact, or even BCC from Gmail)
2. Paste the Google ad into your Google Ads account under a new responsive search ad
3. Schedule the Facebook ad copy for a new campaign targeting your zip codes + homeowner interest
4. Post the Nextdoor copy in your local neighborhood groups (business page OR personal, tag your service area)
5. Send door hanger copy to a local printer (Vistaprint, 48HourPrint, or local print shop) — typical run: 500 for $85-120
